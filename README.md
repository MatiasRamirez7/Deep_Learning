# Abstract

*Analysis of Visual Characteristics and Elements in Images Using Deep Learning to Recognize and Predict the Emotions Conveyed by Advertising Images*

<p align="justify">
Advertising has evolved continuously across cultures, historical periods, and social contexts, progressing from simple textual formats to increasingly complex audiovisual representations. As consumer preferences and social trends have changed, marketing strategies have also adapted in order to enhance attention, attraction, and emotional engagement.

Concurrently, recent technological advances have made it possible to segment consumers, analyze their behavior, understand their purchase decisions, and even predict them. Within this context, the present thesis contributes to the intersection of visual marketing analysis and artificial intelligence by examining the potential of Deep Learning models as complementary tools for understanding customer needs and preferences.

To address this objective, this research focuses on Deep Learning, a subfield of Machine Learning characterized by its ability to learn representations directly from data through training processes based on large numbers of labeled examples. Given sufficient data, deep neural networks can generalize learned patterns and apply them to new, previously unseen instances.

Accordingly, this study investigates whether deep neural network models can be used to address two relevant questions in marketing research: which advertising images generate greater emotional attraction, and which visual characteristics explain such attractiveness. These questions provide the conceptual basis for the development of this thesis.
</p>
